‚ôø Accessibility Service Disclosure

Google Play Compliance Statement

Why FlowBit Requires Accessibility Service

FlowBit uses the Android Accessibility Service exclusively for the following purposes:

  • Detecting app activity: To identify when you open apps or content that you've chosen to block (such as YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, or specific apps).
  • Displaying block overlay: To show a blocking screen when you attempt to access blocked content, helping you stay focused.

What Data the Accessibility Service Receives

  • App package names (e.g., "com.google.android.youtube")
  • Window state change events (e.g., when an app is opened or closed)
  • UI component identifiers (to detect specific screens like Shorts or Reels)

What FlowBit Does NOT Access

FlowBit NEVER reads, logs, stores, or transmits:

  • ‚ùå Text content displayed on your screen
  • ‚ùå Personal messages, emails, or chat content
  • ‚ùå Passwords, payment information, or credentials
  • ‚ùå Keyboard input or touch gestures
  • ‚ùå Audio, photos, or any media files
  • ‚ùå Any data to external servers

Data Processing

All accessibility events are processed entirely on your device and are never uploaded, sold, or shared with anyone. The data is used only in real-time to determine if blocking should be triggered.
